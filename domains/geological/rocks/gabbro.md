# Gabbro

## Assembly Properties
- **Assembly Index**: 240
- **Domain**: Geological
- **Origin Time**: 4.0 billion years ago
- **Copy Number**: Common plutonic rock

## Description

Gabbro is a coarse-grained mafic igneous rock with assembly index 240, the plutonic equivalent of basalt formed by slow cooling of mafic magma.

## Assembly Process
- **Building Blocks**: Plagioclase, pyroxene, olivine, hornblende
- **Formation**: Slow subsurface cooling
- **Environment**: Layered intrusions, ophiolites

## Mineral Composition
- **Plagioclase**: 40-80% (labradorite-bytownite)
- **Pyroxene**: 20-60% (augite, hypersthene)
- **Olivine**: 0-40%
- **Hornblende**: Alteration product

## Types
- **Olivine Gabbro**: Olivine-bearing
- **Leucogabbro**: Light-colored, feldspar-rich
- **Troctolite**: Olivine-plagioclase rock
- **Anorthosite**: >90% plagioclase

## Formation Settings
- **Layered Intrusions**: Bushveld, Stillwater
- **Ocean Crust**: Layer 3 of oceanic crust
- **Continental Intrusions**: Mafic plutons
- **Ophiolite Complexes**: Ancient ocean crust

## Economic Importance
- **Construction**: Dimension stone, aggregate
- **Ni-Cu-PGE**: Ore-bearing intrusions
- **Chromite**: Associated chromium ores
- **Vanadium**: Ti-V magnetite ores
