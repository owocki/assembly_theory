The Recursive Threshold

Life represents the moment when physics crosses a critical threshold: when physical processes become capable of creating models of themselves and using those models to modify their own behavior. This is fundamentally different from simple feedback loops.

Key characteristics of recursive physics:

  • Self-Model: The system contains a representation of itself
  • Self-Modification: It can change its own structure based on this model
  • Bootstrap Loop: Improvements enable better self-modeling
  • Open-Ended: No upper limit to complexity growth

This recursion is what makes life so different from non-living matter and explains why biological systems can achieve such extraordinary complexity.
