The Risk Multiplication Effect

When smart contracts compose, their risks don't simply add—they multiply. Assembly theory provides a framework to understand and quantify this multiplication effect, revealing how seemingly secure individual protocols can create systemic vulnerabilities when combined.

Compositional Risk Formula

Risk(A∘B) = Risk(A) × Risk(B) × Interaction_Complexity × External_Dependencies

Where ∘ represents protocol composition

This multiplicative nature explains why DeFi hacks often occur at protocol boundaries rather than within individual protocols. The assembly index helps predict where these vulnerabilities are most likely to emerge.

Risk Mitigation Through Assembly Theory

1. Modular Isolation

Design protocols with clear boundaries and minimal cross-dependencies. Each module should have a low individual AI to reduce compositional complexity.

2. Assembly Index Limits

Set maximum AI thresholds for protocol integrations. Refuse compositions that exceed critical complexity levels (e.g., combined AI > 300,000).

3. Progressive Decoupling

As protocols grow in complexity, actively work to decouple components. This reduces the multiplication effect of compositional risks.

4. Risk-Weighted Audits

Allocate security resources based on assembly indices. High-AI interaction points deserve exponentially more scrutiny than low-AI components.

Quantitative Risk Scoring

Assembly theory enables precise risk quantification for DeFi compositions:

DeFi Risk Score Calculation

Risk_Score = log(Combined_AI) × Interaction_Count × External_Dependencies

Score ranges: 0-50 (Low), 50-100 (Medium), 100-200 (High), 200+ (Critical)

This quantitative approach transforms risk assessment from subjective analysis to objective measurement, enabling better decision-making for both developers and users.
