The Deassembly Principle

In Assembly Theory, death is simply the reversal of assembly. Living systems maintain their high Assembly Index by constantly fighting entropy through energy input. When this energy flow stops, the complex structures can no longer maintain themselves.

Key aspects of deassembly:

  • Thermodynamic Inevitability: Without energy input, complex structures must simplify
  • Information Loss: The specific arrangements that created function are erased
  • Component Preservation: Atoms are immortal; only their arrangements change
  • Complexity Return: High Assembly Index returns to the cosmic pool
  • Future Potential: Deassembled components enable new assembly elsewhere

This perspective reveals death not as destruction but as transformation‚Äîcomplexity returning to simplicity, making future complexity possible. Every death funds future life.
