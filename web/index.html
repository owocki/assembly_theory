Assembly Theory Web Applications

Assembly Theory provides a unified framework for understanding how complexity emerges across all scales of existence - from atoms to organisms to technologies. It quantifies complexity through the "Assembly Index" (AI), which measures the minimum number of steps required to construct an object from its basic components. This approach reveals that seemingly different phenomena - like the evolution of flight in insects, birds, and aircraft, or the independent development of vision systems - follow similar assembly principles despite arising through completely different pathways.

The power of Assembly Theory lies in its ability to predict which complex structures are likely to emerge, identify convergent evolution patterns, and bridge gaps between physics, biology, and technology. By mapping the "assembly space" of possible configurations, we can understand why certain solutions appear repeatedly across different domains, anticipate future evolutionary or technological developments, and recognize the fundamental constraints that shape complexity in our universe.

This page contains web-based applications and visualizations for exploring these Assembly Theory concepts through interactive tools and data visualizations.
